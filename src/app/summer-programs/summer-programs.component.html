2018 SUMMER CAMPS


During the summer of 2018, Colorado Ballet Academy will offer Summer Dance Camps for Ages 3-4, 5-6, 7-9. All classes will take place at the Colorado Ballet Studios at the Armstrong Center for Dance, 1075 Santa Fe Drive, Denver, CO.  Please view the 2018 Summer Camp schedule below and click register to sign up for a specific camp.

Boys and Girls ages 3-4 & 5-6

Ballet Prince & Princess Camp | July 30-August 3

Time: 9:30a.m.-12:00p.m.
Price: $175

The classic prince and princesses featured prominently in the world of ballet come to life in this camp. The Sleeping Beauty, Swan Lake, Cinderella, and The Nutcracker are some of the ballets explored.

Ballet Elf & Fairy Camp | August 6-10

Time: 9:30a.m.-12:00p.m.
Price: $175

Elves, fairies, imps and pixies inhabit many classical ballets from Sleeping Beauty to Midsummer Night’s Dream.

In both ballet camps the dancers will practice age and skill appropriate ballet movements and skills, learn about the music, have story time, and make art and crafts inspired by these mischievous characters.

Boys and Girls Ages 7-9

Dance Around the World — East | July 30-August 3
Dance Around the World — West | August 6-August 10

Time: 9:00a.m.-1:30p.m.
Price: $275

North, South, East & West! Every country has traditional and classical dance forms. In addition to daily ballet classes, students will learn and practice other popular dance forms from around the world. Eastern dances may include Chinese, Flamenco, and African. Western dances may include Break Dancing, Hip-Hop, Square Dance, and Salsa. Each week will conclude with a demonstration of what the students learned. A trip around the world through dance!

Past Classes

Move, Make & Create – Classical | June 4-8

Time: 9:30a.m.-12:00p.m.
Price: $175

Tchaikovsky, Monet and Marius Petipa! The dancers will be introduced and immersed in all that is classical in the worlds of ballet, music and art! A week of ballet to the classics – The Sleeping Beauty, The Nutcracker and Swan Lake. Classical costumes, classical art, stage design and crafts, all combined to expand creativity and imagination. The week will culminate in a short, informal presentation of all that they learned and created.
